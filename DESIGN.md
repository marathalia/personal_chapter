## Overview

Replica Personal Chapter is an editorial luxury fragrance interface. It should feel like a Maison Margiela Replica label translated into a mobile product: ivory paper, black ink, warm amber details, archival cards, and restrained technology. The product is not loud or glossy. It is tactile, quiet, precise, and intimate.

The app centers on a smart fragrance journey: memories become scent direction, Decode turns skin chemistry into readable data, Explore works like a fragrance archive, and Home keeps the physical device close to the user. The design should always make these ideas feel premium rather than clinical.

The visual language combines three recurring motifs:

- **Replica label language:** uppercase micro-labels, tight tracking, black ink, and paper-like backgrounds.
- **Luxury device language:** soft rounded forms, glassy ivory surfaces, subtle shadows, and cartridge silhouettes.
- **Archive/editorial language:** black-and-white memory cards, dark hero panels, grid texture, and curated scent-story cards.

## Colors

The palette is warm, desaturated, and paper-based. Pure white should be rare. The primary canvas is ivory, with darker black-brown ink for hierarchy and warm amber for emphasis.

- **Ivory and paper surfaces** create the base of the app. Use `surface`, `paper`, `mist`, and `surface-dim` instead of flat white.
- **Ink and smoke** create premium contrast. Use `ink`, `charcoal`, and `smoke` for hero panels, primary buttons, and high-emphasis text.
- **Amber gold** is the signature accent. Use it sparingly for labels, active states, dividers, scan highlights, and selected accord details.
- **Muted accord colors** should feel like perfume materials, not app category colors. They can distinguish scent families, but should stay dusty and controlled.
- **Avoid bright blue, saturated green, and generic purple.** If cool colors appear, they should be softened into sage, stone, or grayscale archive photography.

## Typography

Typography is sans-serif only. It should feel clean, editorial, and label-like. Use Helvetica Neue or a close neutral sans-serif.

Large headings should be confident but not childish. They use heavy weights, tight line heights, and negative tracking. Micro-labels are uppercase, widely tracked, and often amber or gray. Body copy is quiet, compact, and warm gray.

Use typography this way:

- **Display:** reserved for `Personal Chapter`, major screen titles, and hero moments.
- **Headlines:** used inside dark hero cards and major cards.
- **Body:** used for scent explanations, profile copy, and educational text.
- **Micro-labels:** used for `CURRENT BLEND`, `SCAN READS`, `SCENT LIBRARY`, `MEMORY ACCORDS`, and other Replica-label moments.

Do not introduce decorative serif, script, or playful display type. The luxury feeling should come from composition, spacing, contrast, and restraint.

## Layout

The app is mobile-first inside a rounded phone frame. Content is vertically scrollable, but important actions should be close to the top of each screen. Avoid burying core flows behind multiple intro cards.

Use a 4px micro-grid with an 8px practical rhythm. Standard page padding is 24px. Cards generally use 16px to 18px internal padding. Gaps should feel deliberate, not crowded.

Layout principles:

- **Home answers what to do next.** Device status and the next step should be immediately visible.
- **Memory feels like an archive.** Saved memories should use tall visual cards with a monochrome image panel and a white label strip.
- **Explore feels like a scent library.** Accord families can sit in a side rail, while individual accords become large scent-story cards.
- **Lab is functional but curated.** It should show selected accords and fit results early, without forcing users through long forms.
- **Profile is diagnostic.** It should reveal what the app knows, but should not show scent conclusions before memory or Decode input exists.

Use subtle grid textures in hero panels and backgrounds. Grid lines should be low opacity and never compete with content.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is soft, ambient, and paper-like. Avoid harsh drop shadows, thick borders, and overly glossy effects.

Elevation should feel like layered paper and frosted packaging:

- **Base layer:** ivory paper background with subtle gradient and grid texture.
- **Card layer:** translucent ivory glass cards with thin warm borders.
- **Hero layer:** dark ink/smoke panels with grid overlay and amber orbital lines.
- **Modal layer:** bottom sheets with ivory content and dark editorial headers.
- **Frame layer:** the phone frame floats over a deep brown-black browser background.

Use backdrop blur on navigation and glass cards when possible, but keep opacity high enough for legibility. Shadows should be wide and low-opacity, with warm brown rather than pure black.

## Shapes

The shape system is soft but not bubbly. Corners are rounded enough to feel premium and tactile, while still preserving Replica’s structured label aesthetic.

- **Phone frame:** 48px radius.
- **Hero cards:** 28px radius.
- **Glass cards:** 22px radius.
- **Compact cards and controls:** 12px to 18px radius.
- **Pills and badges:** full radius.
- **Archive memory cards:** mostly square, with minimal or no rounding, to preserve the Maison Margiela archive-reference feeling.

Use circles sparingly for status dots, cartridge controls, battery details, and soft scan/device illustrations.

## Components

### App Frame

The app sits inside a tall rounded mobile frame. The frame uses ivory gradients and a faint grid texture. The outside browser/background is deep brown-black to make the phone feel like an object.

### Dark Hero Card

Dark hero cards are the main premium visual device. They use black-brown gradients, subtle grid lines, amber accent labels, and a small device/cartridge illustration. Use them for Home, Explore, Decode, Blend/Device, and other major narrative sections.

### Glass Card

Glass cards are used for secondary content: progress, scan facts, profile details, forms, and compact summaries. They should be ivory-tinted, softly elevated, and bordered with a thin warm outline.

### Buttons

Primary buttons are filled black-brown with ivory uppercase text. Secondary buttons are ivory with thin warm borders and black-brown text. Button labels should be short, uppercase, and spaced.

Do not use multiple unrelated button styles on the same screen. A screen should have one clear primary action.

### Bottom Navigation

Navigation is a floating frosted ivory bar with four tabs: Home, Memory, Explore, Profile. Icons should be thin geometric line icons. The active tab uses a subtle filled background and stronger black text.

### Memory Archive Cards

Memory cards should look like Replica memory references: monochrome photographic panels, white caption strips, uppercase labels, and small amber selection/check marks. The emotional or accord details can be tucked below or revealed as a secondary layer, but the first read should be visual and archival.

### Accord Story Cards

Accord cards should feel like fragrance vignettes, not plain database rows. Use family-specific atmospheric backgrounds, dark overlays, large scent names, family labels, notes, and actions for Preview and Use in Lab.

### Device Card

Device cards show the physical cartridge system. The cartridge silhouette is ivory with three vertical accord chambers. Battery should only appear after pairing. Ratios remain adjustable with clear `-`, `%`, and `+` controls.

### Decode / Scan Cards

Decode is an in-store action. It should clearly explain that the scan reads five biological inputs: pH, microbiome, temperature, hydration, and sebum/oil. These should be visible as separate scan tokens, not merged into one vague card.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Use ivory, ink, amber, taupe, stone, and muted accord colors.
- Keep labels uppercase, tracked, and precise.
- Use dark editorial hero cards to make important sections feel premium.
- Make memory and accord browsing visual, not just form-like.
- Keep the physical device visible and understandable.
- Keep copy short and useful.
- Make state logic honest: no battery before pairing, no Skin Fit before Decode, no profile conclusion before input.

### Don't

- Do not use bright random category colors unless they are carefully muted.
- Do not make every card a plain white rounded rectangle.
- Do not over-explain the same concept on every tab.
- Do not bury important actions behind long scrolling sections.
- Do not use decorative fonts, script fonts, or childish icon-heavy layouts.
- Do not show scent match, profile, or skin data before the user has completed the required step.
- Do not make the app feel like a generic wellness dashboard; it should feel like a luxury fragrance archive with smart technology inside it.
